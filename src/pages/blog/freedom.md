I was here for a week in New York (well, Jersey City, but at the risk of offending everyone who's counting!). "was" is the keyword here. I am likely going to stay a bit longer (as is permitted by my ESTA) and travel to California (thanks to one of my friends for putting me up!). I have genuinely never felt so safe and welcome in a country, and as a British person I thought I'd give my two cents on everything. Also no, I'm not here for the royals (as 4 people have asked so far lol)! [If you're after pictures, this is my imgur gallery, Manhattan is stunning (note if you are in the UK you will need a VPN to see this lol)!](https://imgur.com/gallery/manhattan-pictures-CMU3Yl1)

I am actively looking for sponsorship opportunities in the US (minus some red states)! Please let me know if you can help!

## My initial arrival

Pre-clearance is pretty cool. I was pre-cleared for travel at Dublin Airport, and after that I didn't have to worry about any further queues. Super cool!

I flew Delta. The wireless was non-existent on the flight even though it said it existed, but the food was genuinely magnificent. I had multiple drinks on the flight, and the curry they gave me was genuinely amazing. One of the best dishes I had had for a while! It didn't feel like 7 hours, it was amazing. I had genuinely never been in a plane the size of that one!

The airport shuttle was my first taste of the outside of this beautiful city, and it was beautiful! The bit between the airport and where I took the bus wasn't as great to be honest. The carriage was clean, but needing to use the magstripe on my card was annoying. This is something that is extroadinarily uncommon in Europe due to credit card theft. The bus network is also not ideal between NY and NJ. It felt like that bit in The Onion where to vote you need to pull a million levers.

Once I had figured everything out and was in Jersey City, my phone was on like 6% battery. Unfamiliar with street numbers, I asked someone where it would be. This was my first taste of the American people within their neighbourhoods. I was expecting like you'd get in the UK some sort of vague pointer and quick walkaway. This person actively took time of their day to both get to know me and also get me to the place. I was so, so thankful for that. It made me realise that this level of kindness is free and is something that everyone should show.

## Nobody cares who I am (and I love it!)

This is a hard one to quantify but in the UK, I am so incredibly used to my identity being used as a weapon. It kind of changed how I looked at the world because I felt the entire world was like this. To be honest, I thought it would be extremely bad in the US. The president is not super pro my existence or anything like that. And sure, this is an enormous country. I am certain some people buy into that. I can safely say in NYC or Jersey City I have not had a single person stare at me, make a comnent to me, or be horrible to me. The American people have shown me nothing but deep kindness.

This should be normal. Like this shouldn't even be a point that I am treated normal. But I did not for one second expect that. I was expecting like the UK that everyone would have an opinion on my existence, and they just don't! I guess that's why it was so confusing to me for a long time that Americans are less politically loud by in large. I'm used to this shit being my life. I do not feel I need to advocate to be treated human here, and it means a lot. Thank you New York!

A lot of what I thought America would be like is just plain wrong, and this is the safest country/state I have ever been in! Seeing neighbours be friends and not just knock on others doors to tell them to turn the music down is beautiful, the people are amazing, the food is amazing, and the views are fantastic. I really want to call this state my home!
